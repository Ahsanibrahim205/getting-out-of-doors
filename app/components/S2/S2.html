Why you may need
more exposure to daylight

  • It seems that one in five adults in Northern Europe and North America will experience symptoms of cyclical depression, coupled with anxiety bouts, as a result of insufficient exposure to daylight.
  • This happens particularly during the darker months from November to March (some suggest that this ‘season’ is even longer: September to April – i.e. 8 out of the 12 months).
  • SAD does not seem just be confined to winter months, it has been observed in individuals who
    1. spend large proportions of their time indoors
    2. work in offices at locations away from windows
  • These people are spending large parts of their day without any, or much, direct sunlight.
  • If sometimes you are having the types of wellness problems that are covered in this Wellness Development Activity, such as loss of concentration or feelings of low energy you should start A Wellness Diary.

    • For two weeks note when these occur how long, if at all, you have had a “bath of daylight” that day? You will be able to take action based on your findings.
