Why daylight is so important for your wellness

  • Insufficient daylight may cause psychological health problems, in certain people, because of its effect on the production of the hormone melatonin.
  • Melatonin is released from the pineal gland (a pea-size structure at the centre of the brain) after several hours of darkness and it is presumed to be one of the bodyâ€™s chemicals involved in causing us to feel sleepy.
  • When daylight comes, the brain inhibits the secretion of melatonin and the period of wakefulness begins.
  • Evidence suggests that individuals with SAD have a longer period of melatonin production at night in the winter. In addition, SAD sufferers may have higher daytime melatonin levels during winter, compared to those without SAD.
  • Alternative theories suggest that it is the lack of serotonin, which is a characteristic of people in depressive states.
  • Serotonin is produced when adequate light is available.

    • The negative impact of insufficient daylight on psychological health has been termed: seasonal affective disorder (SAD).



    Actions you can take

  • Your knowing that daylight is associated to varying degrees with the mental wellbeing of an individual should make you more conscious of the need to make daily efforts to take a dosage of sunlight whenever possible.
  • The most obvious and cheap solution is, each day, to take a bath of daylight for about 15 to 30 minutes or at least seek out whatever daylight is available
  • You can buy special lights to provide this daylight effect indoors.
  • These lights offer generally about 10 to 50 times the intensity of normal indoor lighting.
  • Exposure to the light of these lamps is usually recommended to be in sessions of about 30 minutes with a maximum time of around 2 hours per day
  • Certainly consulting a doctor, or other specialised health professional, is highly recommended if you believe you might be experiencing SAD symptoms to such an extent that you are feeling negative about your wellness levels.
